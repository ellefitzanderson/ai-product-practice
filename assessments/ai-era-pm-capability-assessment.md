# AI-Era PM Capability Assessment

A diagnostic for where a product person actually sits on the spectrum from *"has opinions about AI"* to *"ships with it daily."* Use it on yourself honestly, or as a manager-as-coach conversation frame — it is a stretching tool, not a grading tool.

**How to score:** For each dimension, pick the level that describes what you *did in the last 60 days* — not what you understand, agree with, or plan. Capability is behaviour under recency.

---

## Dimension 1 — Daily practice

| Level | Behaviour in the last 60 days |
|---|---|
| 1 | Used a chatbot to draft the odd email |
| 2 | Regular drafting/summarising; no reusable patterns |
| 3 | Maintains personal prompt patterns; knows where they fail |
| 4 | Has automated a recurring workflow end-to-end (with review step) |
| 5 | Builds tools others use — GPTs, skills, scripts — and maintains them |

## Dimension 2 — Product judgement about AI

| Level | Behaviour |
|---|---|
| 1 | Evaluates AI features by demo impressiveness |
| 2 | Asks about accuracy; accepts a single number as the answer |
| 3 | Asks about failure states, confidence thresholds, and the moment after a wrong answer |
| 4 | Frames evals as a product artefact; has defined acceptable-error for a real feature |
| 5 | Has killed or descoped an AI feature for trust/economics reasons and can defend the call |

## Dimension 3 — Economics

| Level | Behaviour |
|---|---|
| 1 | Believes AI features are roughly free to run |
| 2 | Knows tokens exist |
| 3 | Can estimate per-interaction cost of a feature and how it scales |
| 4 | Has made a routing/caching/model-choice decision to change unit economics |
| 5 | Owns an AI feature P&L view; cost is on the same dashboard as adoption |

## Dimension 4 — Working with the technical team

| Level | Behaviour |
|---|---|
| 1 | Hands requirements over the wall; "the data scientists do the AI part" |
| 2 | Attends model reviews; understands the headlines |
| 3 | Can interrogate an eval result and ask for the missing slice |
| 4 | Co-designs the eval set with engineering; brings the customer-impact weighting |
| 5 | Team treats them as the authority on *what wrong answers cost*, and plans around it |

## Dimension 5 — Organisational leadership

| Level | Behaviour |
|---|---|
| 1 | Forwards AI articles |
| 2 | Ran a lunch-and-learn |
| 3 | Has changed one team ritual to use AI well (and measured whether it helped) |
| 4 | Sets team norms: where AI is mandatory, optional, and banned — with reasons |
| 5 | Has built org-level capability: training, tooling, or standards adopted beyond their team |

---

## Reading your profile

- **Mostly 1–2:** You have opinions. That's fine — eighteen months ago it was normal. It is no longer normal. Pick one recurring task and build a pattern for it this week.
- **Spiky (a 4 and some 1s):** Common and honest. The gap between your strong and weak dimensions is your interview risk — that's where the probing question lands.
- **Mostly 3:** Solidly practising. The move to 4 is always the same: stop consuming, start *owning* — a workflow, an eval, a cost decision.
- **Mostly 4–5:** Your evidence should be public. If someone asked "show me," what link would you send? If there's no link, build the link.

## Manager-as-coach usage

Don't hand this over as homework. Pick **one** dimension per 1:1, ask for the last-60-days story, and agree the single next behaviour. Capable people don't need the whole grid at once — they need one honest stretch at a time.
